About

Penned somewhere between 14 and 40 AD, Marcus Manilius’s Astronomica acts as the Western world’s debut concept album on the cosmos—and stands as our earliest surviving comprehensive treatise on astrology. Spread across five sweeping "books" of Latin didactic poetry, the work completely ignores the modern divide between science and mysticism. Instead, Manilius flawlessly mixes the two, treating the cold, hard math of celestial geometry as the necessary baseline to decode the stars' divine influence on our lives. Heavy with Stoic philosophy, this epic paints the universe not as a random void, but as a single, breathing organism perfectly orchestrated by immutable fate and divine reason (ratio).
